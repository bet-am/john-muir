A Wind Storm in

the Forests

by John Muir

the winds go to every tree, fingering every leaf and branch
pines six feet in diameter bending like grasses
noble tree-waving and tree-music
setting free a steam of summery fragrance
varying tones of individual trees
wild sea of pines
branches and boles booming like waterfalls
descends in a rush of waterlike ripples
trees are travelers
